Here's an interesting fact: Did you know that honey never spoils? Archaeologists have found pots of honey in ancient Egyptian tombs that are over 3,000 years old and still perfectly edible.

This is because of the unique chemical composition of honey:

Low moisture content: Honey is naturally low in moisture, which prevents bacteria and microorganisms from growing.
High acidity: Honey has a pH between 3 and 4.5, making it acidic enough to kill most bacteria.
Hydrogen peroxide: When bees deposit nectar into honeycombs, an enzyme called glucose oxidase breaks down glucose and generates hydrogen peroxide, a well-known antibacterial agent.
Sugar concentration: Honey is a supersaturated solution of sugars, primarily glucose and fructose. This high sugar content creates a hypertonic environment that dehydrates bacteria, effectively killing them.
These factors work together to make honey a natural preservative. As long as it's stored in a sealed container to prevent moisture from getting in, honey can last for centuries or even millennia without spoiling. It's a remarkable testament to the ingenuity of bees and the unique properties of this sweet substance they produce.
