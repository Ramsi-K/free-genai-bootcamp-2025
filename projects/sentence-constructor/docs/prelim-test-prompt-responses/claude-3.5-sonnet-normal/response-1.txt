I find it fascinating how octopuses are essentially living examples of distributed intelligence. Unlike most animals, including humans, who have centralized brains, octopuses have a significant portion of their neurons spread throughout their arms. Each arm has its own "mini-brain" that can process information and control movement independently. In fact, about 2/3 of an octopus's neurons are in its arms rather than its central brain.

This decentralized nervous system allows octopuses to perform incredibly complex manipulations with their arms without having to consciously control each movement. They can even solve puzzles and manipulate objects with their arms while their central brain is focused on something else entirely.
